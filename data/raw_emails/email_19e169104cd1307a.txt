Consultancy services were provided across multiple programmes, encompassing
nonconformity resolution, test plan development, grease application
guidance, supplier diversification support, and participation in the ESA
NESSRI slip ring activity. The MARA pinion gear nonconformity was assessed
and the batch was approved for use despite surface roughness exceeding
specification. Grease plating applied three or more times was recommended
to mitigate cold welding risk. A change to the nominal roughness
specification was advised against to preserve MoS2 coating performance. The
MARA test plan was finalised and the observation requirements were issued
via Excel. The EQM test plan proposal was approved, with motor temperature
reading set at 1 Hz and microvibration measurements designated optional. A
syringe-based grease application method was proposed for large surface
areas. The initial grease plating trial parameters were reviewed, covering
a 2:1 solvent-to-grease ratio, brush application, and 12-hour curing at
60°C. Inspection strategies for non-disassemblable pinion bearings were
discussed and clarification on application method and oven dwelling time
was requested. The tooth flank area was confirmed at 26.4 mm2 for lubricant
mass calculation. Supplier diversification for ZARA coatings was supported
following handling issues with Eifeler, and a preliminary alternative
supplier database was initiated. Coating quantity information was requested
to construct a requirement table for negotiations. The ESA NESSRI kick-off
meeting was attended on 20 April, and the action plan, project timeline,
and shared folder were requested. The Gantt chart with a 15-week shift was
reviewed.
