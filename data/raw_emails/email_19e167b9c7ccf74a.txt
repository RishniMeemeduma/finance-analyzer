description :   Consultancy activities for Revolv Space during March 2026
were conducted across two principal workstreams, encompassing technical
nonconformity assessment and test plan finalisation for the MARA programme.
A nonconformity affecting a batch of MoS₂-coated MARA pinion gears was
evaluated, wherein surface roughness values were found to exceed
specification; one component was measured at Ra 0.519 µm against a target
of Ra 0.1 µm, representing a fivefold deviation from ESA/ESTL
recommendations. Technical risk posed by deploying the unidentifiable batch
in Flight Models was assessed. A recommendation was issued on 31 March to
retain the batch for use, with the condition that grease plating be applied
a minimum of three times to ensure adequate lubricant provision. The
rationale was established that fluid lubrication is less sensitive to
surface roughness than dry-film coatings, and that grease would serve as
the primary cold-welding preventative in the event of MoS₂ coating
degradation. Hybrid lubrication behaviour of the system was reviewed to
support this position. In parallel, the MARA Engineering Qualification
Model test plan was completed and delivered on 16 March. Recommendations
for initial condition measurements and tribological baselines, required to
support a potential three-year service life extension, were incorporated.
Updated experimental data  ncluding motor torque and EM twist capsule
friction torque measurements  were received from the project team following
a meeting held on 18 March and were integrated into the tribological
analysis table. Coordination activities included multiple scheduling
exchanges to align review meetings around the test plan and nonconformity
discussions, with a session on 18 March confirmed with Diego Cremonesi to
address updates in detail.
